Thesis

Multi-strategy at agent scale.

A multi-strategy hedge fund is a federation of independent pods — each a PM, a quant team, a book. Pod count has always tracked headcount.

Multi-agent capability closed that gap in the last six months. Polyvaria runs the same federation with autonomous agent teams in place of seats. Quant and dev pods build the back office and the research stack, and operate them live. Practitioners set the strategy, validate the research, and own the book. Ideas and infrastructure scale at the speed of compute — the ceiling rises with every model release.

  • Pods as agent teams. Practitioners at the helm.
  • R&D and infra at the speed of compute.
  • Asymmetric exposure to model capability.

Approach

The pipeline.

The same code runs a daily rebalance and a multi-year historical replay. The only difference between them is the as-of date.

  1. 01

    Universe

    Survivorship-corrected.

    The investable universe is reconstructed for every historical date from a delisted-companies snapshot. The platform never trades names it could not have known about at the time.

  2. 02

    Signal Agents

    Forty-three independent views.

    Each agent ingests price, fundamental, event, or news data and produces a cross-sectional ranking. Value, momentum, quality, capital discipline, low-risk, reversal, liquidity, post-earnings drift, sentiment.

  3. 03

    Aggregation

    Sector-neutral, factor-residualized.

    Raw signals are winsorized, z-scored within sector, then residualized against the Fama-French factors. What remains is an expected-return vector orthogonal to known risk premia.

  4. 04

    Risk Model

    Five-factor structural.

    Market, size, value, profitability, and investment exposures are estimated with rolling regression. The resulting covariance enters the optimization objective directly, with shrinkage on residual variance.

  5. 05

    Optimizer

    Constrained mean-variance.

    A conic solver maximizes expected return net of risk and transaction cost, subject to long-only, sector, capitalization-bucket, single-name, and turnover constraints. Warm-started for stability across rebalances.

  6. 06

    Execution

    Reconciled and idempotent.

    Target weights become a share-level trade list. Orders carry deterministic identifiers so retries are safe. Holdings are reconciled against broker positions before each rebalance.

Research Library

Fifteen families of signal.

Each strategy is implemented as a standalone agent and validated against its own information coefficient before it earns weight in the live portfolio.

New strategies enter at configuration velocity. Adding a signal, cost model, or constraint does not require changes to the registry or to any code on the trading-critical path.

Principles

Recursive self-improvement.

Every layer of the stack has its own feedback loop. The agents that close those loops get stronger with every model release.

Signals scored against forward returns.

Every cached signal is evaluated daily against its information coefficient over the prediction horizon. Decaying signals are flagged before they earn weight. The machine-learning overlay refits on a walk-forward schedule with an embargo that prevents leakage. Each cycle, new hypotheses enter the cache, evidence accumulates, weights adjust.

Strategy as a search problem.

Factor model, exposure estimator, covariance assembler, constraints, cost model — each component is hot-swappable from configuration. The backtest-replay tooling lets agent pods run counterfactual configurations against history and surface the parameter and component choices that improve attribution.

Execution calibrated by its own fills.

Linear and square-root impact coefficients in the cost model are calibrated against realized broker reconciliation. Holdings are reconciled before every rebalance; a repair routine adjusts for fills that miss. As trade history accumulates, the impact model and the execution layer sharpen with it.

The operational surface widens with model capability.

Pipelines, monitors, data ingest, and the platform code itself are authored and maintained by the same agent pods that operate the system. Each step-up in the underlying models widens what gets built, debugged, and shipped without human intervention. The platform compounds.
